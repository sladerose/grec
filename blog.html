This is an adaptation on an online conversation on Facebook between Brennan Glansbeek and Chris de Klerk, regarding the above picture posted on Gold Reef Equitation Facebook Group and Brennan Glansbeek’s own personal Facebook profile.

The very first thing is what Brennan Glansbeek call fast food training. I.E. let’s get the horse down in front. That is all it is, is down in front. That mind set has no purpose or long-term effectiveness. Down in front is an accepted term and way of moving to please judges and the public in general because it looks cool to the layman but in-fact it is very counterproductive. All that down in front creates is a horse with a locked jaw and no top line usage or top line roundness. This will be where all the resistance starts. A horse that resists in the jaw will not be able to engage its hocks.
The very first step a rider should focus on is movement from behind. In other words, propelling horse forward from the hocks being engaged or backend forward movement. This takes time to achieve with many horses. Together with the hind end forward movement the rider should sponge with his or her ring finger on the rein. This is not a half halt or a big movement. Heavy hands or busy hands create the locked jaw issue again. Sponging is a very subtle movement done with the ring finger. It should be barely visible, but the horse will feel it in his mouth. If the horse is comfortable with the bit, then he will learn to relax the jaw.
To get back to the above picture. One can assume that this rider has hard hands and wants to pull the horse onto the bit per say. We know this to be incorrect. To fix this the horse must become comfortable with the bit in its mouth first. As such no contact riding for a while with sponging on the long rein – the horse can feel this is if done correctly – slowly reteaching horse that the bit is not going to hurt the mouth. One can assume that this horse will still want to travel behind the bit. Raising the rider’s hands will help to pick horses head up. At the same time horses’ hocks need to be re-activated as the engine of the horse would have been shut down due to the excessive pressure in the mouth.
One must not forget there are so many variables that need to be factored in. Horse conformation, rider ability, undoing incorrect training, correct fitting riding equipment etc etc, Above is just a guideline to help fix what we presume is going on in that picture. One thing is for sure every rider should strive to have an uphill travelling horse. That is engaged from behind, working over the round top line with his nose on the vertical line in front. This takes years to develop. The horse must create the correct muscles to carry himself like this. With this training the horse will be what we know as light in front and CORRECTLY between the hand and the leg.
